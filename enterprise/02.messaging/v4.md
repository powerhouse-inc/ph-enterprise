## 2. The Problem

**"Every AI vendor wants your data. Your regulators say no."**

Enterprise AI adoption is stalling — not because the technology isn't ready, but because the deployment model is wrong. Commercial AI services require sending your data to external infrastructure for processing, fine-tuning, or retrieval. For organizations handling privileged legal communications, employee PII, financial forecasts, or procurement negotiations, that's not a risk assessment — it's a non-starter.

The result: teams that urgently need AI capabilities are stuck with either consumer-grade chatbots that can't touch real business data, or year-long integration projects that bolt AI onto systems never designed for it.

There's a third path: infrastructure that makes your business data AI-native *without it ever leaving your environment*.

*Reframes driver 3 (data readiness) through the lens of driver 4 (data privacy). The blocker isn't just "data isn't structured" — it's "we can't structure it in a place we don't control."*

## 3. The Solution

**"Private data. AI-native. Real-time."**

Three pillars, presented as one integrated architecture — not a feature list.

### Private by default

Your business data stays on your infrastructure — always. AI models run locally on your hardware (open-source models, your GPUs) for sensitive workflows. Commercial APIs are available for general-purpose tasks, with architectural separation ensuring confidential data never reaches external services. Self-host the entire stack, or use Vetra Cloud managed hosting with data sovereignty guarantees. No data is used for model training. No vendor ever sees your content.

*This is the lead capability. For legal, HR, finance, and procurement — the industries we're targeting — this is the prerequisite that unlocks everything else.*

### AI-native from the data layer up

Every business object in the system automatically exposes GraphQL and MCP endpoints. AI agents interact with your operations through the same typed interfaces as human users — no integration layer, no custom adapters per system. Multi-agent workflows coordinate across business functions: a contract review agent, an approval agent, and a compliance agent can operate on the same data simultaneously, each sandboxed to its own permissions and scope. Agents follow structured process playbooks — specification-driven AI, not open-ended prompts — so you control what they can do, what they can see, and how they escalate.

*AI is the first technical capability described, not an add-on section. Multi-agent workflows and sandboxing are enterprise-grade AI concepts the CTO is evaluating across vendors.*

### Real-time and scalable

Built on a reactive architecture inspired by proven patterns: event sourcing, CQRS, event-driven processing. Business data synchronizes across devices, applications, and users in real-time. The write layer scales horizontally — each business object is an independent consistency boundary. Read models are built by event-driven processors into any datastore you need: relational databases, search indexes, warehouses, caches. Platform-grade performance without centralized infrastructure.

*"Reactive Document Architecture" is the internal name for this. Externally, it's described by what it delivers: real-time sync, horizontal scale, event-driven processing. The enterprise architect recognizes the patterns without learning new vocabulary.*

## 4. The Platform

**"Six components. Each independent. Stronger together."**

*Components reordered: AI-first (Clint), then APIs (Switchboard), then workspace (Connect), then platform-building (Fusion), then ecosystem (Vetra), then identity (Renown). The ordering reflects what the CTO cares about most.*

### Clint — AI Agent Infrastructure

The AI operations layer. Define business processes as typed schemas; Clint generates a CLI, an interactive operator console, an MCP server, and a multi-agent harness — all from one codebase. Agents operate through a routine loop: a continuous execution engine that monitors business events, evaluates triggers, and executes operations autonomously. Each agent runs sandboxed with scoped permissions — access only to the data and operations explicitly granted. Supports local model deployment for confidential workflows and commercial APIs for general tasks, switchable without code changes.

### Switchboard — API & Integration

The system boundary. Exposes every business operation as GraphQL queries/mutations and MCP tool endpoints. External applications, automation workflows, and AI agents all interact through this layer. Handles authorization, real-time sync, and multi-tenant access. Standards-based integration — any system that speaks GraphQL or MCP connects without custom adapters.

### Connect — Operator Workspace

The human interface. A browser-based workspace for creating, editing, and collaborating on business data. Workflow-specific views overlay the data with specialized interfaces — standardized processes directly available rather than generic file management. Operators and AI agents work on the same data; human oversight of AI actions is built into the workspace.

### Fusion — Custom Platforms

Build branded, customer-facing applications on top of your data infrastructure. Next.js-based, Switchboard-backed, fully customizable. Deploy the equivalent of a SaaS product — with the backend entirely on your infrastructure, your data never leaving your environment.

### Vetra — Platform Ecosystem

Three pillars:

- **Packages** — A registry of reusable business modules, workflow editors, data processors, and agent configurations. Pre-built for common domains (finance, legal, HR, procurement). Use as-is, customize, or publish your own.
- **Hosting** — Managed cloud infrastructure with data sovereignty guarantees and real-time sync. Production deployment without hyperscaler dependency.
- **AI Agents** — Pre-configured business process agents. Deploy from the ecosystem or build custom agents with Clint.

Development tooling (Vetra Studio), documentation (Vetra Docs), and training (Vetra Academy) support teams building on the stack.

### Renown — Identity & Reputation

Verified contributor profiles and contribution tracking across your organization and partner networks. The identity layer for authorization, audit trails, and cross-organizational collaboration.

## 5. Data Privacy & Compliance

**"Built into the architecture. Not bolted on."**

*Elevated from a guardrail sub-panel (v3) to its own section. This is the section the CTO sends to the DPO, legal counsel, and compliance team.*

### Data residency

All business data is stored on infrastructure you control. Self-host on your own servers, or deploy on Vetra Cloud with contractual data residency guarantees. No data replication to third-party systems unless you explicitly configure it.

### AI model isolation

Sensitive workflows run on local open-source models deployed on your hardware. Confidential data — personnel records, legal communications, financial projections, negotiation positions — never reaches an external API. General-purpose AI tasks can optionally use commercial providers, architecturally separated from confidential data paths. Switch providers without code changes.

### Agent sandboxing

Each AI agent operates within a defined permission scope: which data it can access, which operations it can perform, which other agents it can communicate with. No agent has blanket access to your systems. Permissions are declarative and auditable.

### Immutable audit trail

Every operation — human or AI — is cryptographically signed, timestamped, and appended to an immutable history. The audit trail is not a log; it *is* the data structure. Who did what, when, why, and with what authority is recorded at the architectural level. SOX, GDPR, HIPAA, industry-specific regulation: compliance is structural, not retrofitted.

### Data portability

Your data is JSON. Your schemas are typed definitions you own. Your application code is TypeScript. If you leave, everything comes with you — no extraction project, no vendor negotiation, no proprietary format to decode. The entire stack is open source.
