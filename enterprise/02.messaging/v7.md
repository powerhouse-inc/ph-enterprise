## 2. The Problem

**"Every AI vendor wants your data. Your regulators say no."**

Enterprise AI adoption is stalling — not because the technology isn't ready, but because the deployment model is wrong. Commercial AI services require sending your data to external infrastructure for processing, fine-tuning, or retrieval. For organizations handling privileged legal communications, employee PII, financial forecasts, or procurement negotiations, that's not a risk assessment — it's a non-starter.

The result: teams that urgently need AI capabilities are stuck with either consumer-grade chatbots that can't touch real business data, or year-long integration projects that bolt AI onto systems never designed for it.

There's a third path: infrastructure that makes your business data AI-native *without it ever leaving your environment*. Not by replacing your existing systems — but by adding a structured layer that makes them AI-accessible, on your infrastructure, under your control.

**Funnel link:** "Find out where you stand — get an AI readiness assessment" -> [bai.powerhouse.io](https://bai.powerhouse.io)

## 3. The Solution

**"Private data. AI-native. Real-time."**

### Private by default

Your business data stays on your infrastructure — always. AI models run locally on your hardware for sensitive workflows. Commercial APIs are available for general-purpose tasks, with architectural separation ensuring confidential data never reaches external services. Self-host the entire stack, or use Vetra Cloud with data sovereignty guarantees. No data is used for model training. No vendor ever sees your content.

### AI-native from the data layer up

Your existing business systems — ERP, HRIS, document management, email — connect to a structured data layer through standards-based integrations. Each integration defines exactly what data flows where, creating natural boundaries for AI access. AI agents interact with your operations through typed interfaces: multi-agent workflows coordinate across business functions, each agent sandboxed to its own data scope and permissions. Agents follow structured process playbooks — specification-driven AI, not open-ended prompts — so you control what they can do, what they can see, and how they escalate. Every autonomous operation can require human approval — the system escalates when confidence is low, when thresholds are exceeded, or whenever your process demands it.

*v7 change [#7]: Human oversight sentence added. Addresses driver 2 — "if I move wrong, it's my neck."*

### Real-time and scalable

Built on a reactive architecture inspired by proven patterns: event sourcing, CQRS, event-driven processing. Business data synchronizes across devices, applications, and users in real-time. The write layer scales horizontally — each business object is an independent consistency boundary. Read models are built by event-driven processors into any datastore you need. Platform-grade performance without centralized infrastructure.

## 5. The Platform

**"Five components. Your architecture. Your brand."**

*Local, whitelabel components you own and operate — on your servers, under your control.*

### Clint — AI Agent Infrastructure

The AI operations layer. Define business processes as typed schemas; Clint generates an operator console, an MCP server, and a multi-agent harness — all from one codebase. Agents operate through a routine loop with pluggable triggers. Each agent runs sandboxed with scoped permissions. Supports local model deployment for confidential workflows and commercial APIs for general tasks.

-> [Learn more](subpages/clint.md)

### Fusion — Internal Enterprise Platform

The modern replacement for your intranet portal. A branded internal platform where teams access business data, workflows, dashboards, and AI agent outputs. Customizable per business unit or department. Replaces legacy portals and scattered departmental tools with a unified, AI-integrated workspace on your own data infrastructure.

-> [Learn more](subpages/fusion.md)

### Connect — Operator Workspace

The hands-on workspace for teams that work directly with business data. A browser-based environment for creating, editing, and collaborating on structured business objects. Workflow-specific views present standardized processes directly. Operators and AI agents work on the same data with built-in human oversight.

-> [Learn more](subpages/connect.md)

### Switchboard — API & Integration

The integration hub. Exposes every business operation as GraphQL and MCP endpoints. Connects to your existing systems through standards-based APIs. Each integration defines data scope and access boundaries. Handles authorization, real-time sync, and multi-tenant access.

-> [Learn more](subpages/switchboard.md)

### Renown — Identity for Agents and Users

Unified identity for every actor in the system — human operators, AI agents, external integrations. Every action is attributed to a verified identity with defined permissions. Governs data access, agent scoping, and trust boundaries. The extension point for enterprise SSO integration.

-> [Learn more](subpages/renown.md)

## 6. Integration & Data Isolation

**"Connect everything. Expose nothing."**

### Parallel adoption, not rip-and-replace

Powerhouse doesn't replace your existing systems — it runs alongside them. Switchboard connects your ERP, HRIS, document management, email, and financial systems to a structured data layer where AI can operate. Your existing workflows continue unchanged. The AI-native layer adds capabilities on top — at your pace, under your control.

*v7 change [#2]: "Parallel adoption" framing now leads the section. Reduces perceived risk of adoption.*

### Every integration is a data boundary

Each connection defines a scope: what data enters, what format it takes, and who — human or agent — can access it. An HR agent sees personnel records but not financial forecasts. A procurement agent sees vendor negotiations but not employee data. A legal review agent accesses contract terms but not compensation details. Isolation is defined at the integration level, not layered on as policy.

### Tiered AI model access

Not all data should reach the same AI model. Powerhouse supports graduated access:

- **Local models** for the most sensitive data — privileged legal communications, raw personnel files, board-level financials, trade secrets. Open-source models on your hardware. Nothing leaves your network.
- **Frontier commercial models** for less sensitive but still confidential work — operating on censored summaries, redacted extracts, or pre-approved data shapes. The integration layer controls what reaches the external model.
- **General-purpose commercial models** for non-confidential tasks — drafting, formatting, research, translation.

Your DPO defines the tiers. The architecture enforces them.

## 7. Privacy & Compliance

**"Built into the architecture. Not bolted on."**

*The section the CTO sends to the DPO, legal counsel, and compliance team.*

### Data residency

All business data is stored on infrastructure you control. Self-host on your own servers, or deploy on Vetra Cloud with contractual data residency guarantees. No data replication to third-party systems unless you explicitly configure it.

### Immutable audit trail

Every operation — human or AI — is cryptographically signed, timestamped, and appended to an immutable history. The audit trail is not a log; it *is* the data structure. Who did what, when, and with what authority is recorded at the architectural level. SOX, GDPR, HIPAA, industry-specific regulation: compliance is structural, not retrofitted.

### Ready for what's coming

The EU AI Act and emerging sector-specific AI regulations will require transparency into AI decision-making, data provenance tracking, and algorithmic accountability. Powerhouse's architecture — immutable operation history, cryptographic attribution of every AI action, specification-driven agent behavior — is structurally ready for these requirements. Organizations adopting this infrastructure now won't need to retrofit compliance when regulation arrives. Those bolting AI onto legacy systems will.

*v7 change [#1]: Forward-looking regulatory paragraph. Turns compliance from "we check the box" into "you're future-proof."*

### Data portability

Your data is JSON. Your schemas are typed definitions you own. Your application code is TypeScript. If you leave, everything comes with you. The entire stack is open source.
