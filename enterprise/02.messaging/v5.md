## 2. The Problem

**"Every AI vendor wants your data. Your regulators say no."**

Enterprise AI adoption is stalling — not because the technology isn't ready, but because the deployment model is wrong. Commercial AI services require sending your data to external infrastructure for processing, fine-tuning, or retrieval. For organizations handling privileged legal communications, employee PII, financial forecasts, or procurement negotiations, that's not a risk assessment — it's a non-starter.

The result: teams that urgently need AI capabilities are stuck with either consumer-grade chatbots that can't touch real business data, or year-long integration projects that bolt AI onto systems never designed for it.

There's a third path: infrastructure that makes your business data AI-native *without it ever leaving your environment*.

## 3. The Solution

**"Private data. AI-native. Real-time."**

### Private by default

Your business data stays on your infrastructure — always. AI models run locally on your hardware for sensitive workflows. Commercial APIs are available for general-purpose tasks, with architectural separation ensuring confidential data never reaches external services. Self-host the entire stack, or use Vetra Cloud with data sovereignty guarantees. No data is used for model training. No vendor ever sees your content.

### AI-native from the data layer up

Your existing business systems — ERP, HRIS, document management, email — connect to a structured data layer through standards-based integrations. Each integration defines exactly what data flows where, creating natural boundaries for AI access. AI agents interact with your operations through typed interfaces: multi-agent workflows coordinate across business functions, each agent sandboxed to its own data scope and permissions. Agents follow structured process playbooks — specification-driven AI, not open-ended prompts — so you control what they can do, what they can see, and how they escalate.

### Real-time and scalable

Built on a reactive architecture inspired by proven patterns: event sourcing, CQRS, event-driven processing. Business data synchronizes across devices, applications, and users in real-time. The write layer scales horizontally — each business object is an independent consistency boundary. Read models are built by event-driven processors into any datastore you need. Platform-grade performance without centralized infrastructure.

## 4. The Platform

**"Five components. Your architecture. Your brand."**

*These are the local, whitelabel components you own and operate. They form the architecture that runs inside your environment — on your servers, under your control, branded as your own.*

### Clint — AI Agent Infrastructure

The AI operations layer. Define business processes as typed schemas; Clint generates an operator console, an MCP server, and a multi-agent harness — all from one codebase. Agents operate through a routine loop: a continuous engine that monitors business events, evaluates triggers, and executes operations autonomously. Each agent runs sandboxed with scoped permissions — access only to the data and operations explicitly granted. Supports local model deployment for confidential workflows and commercial APIs for general tasks, switchable without code changes.

### Fusion — Internal Enterprise Platform

The modern replacement for your intranet portal. A Next.js-based internal platform where teams across departments access business data, workflows, dashboards, and AI agent outputs through a branded web application. Switchboard-backed, customizable per business unit or department. Replaces legacy SharePoint-style portals, internal wikis, and scattered departmental tools with a unified, AI-integrated workspace built on your own data infrastructure. Can also serve external-facing applications when needed.

### Connect — Operator Workspace

The hands-on workspace for teams that work directly with business data. A browser-based environment for creating, editing, and collaborating on structured business objects. Workflow-specific views present standardized processes directly — rather than generic file management. Operators and AI agents work on the same data; human oversight of AI actions is built into the workspace.

### Switchboard — API & Integration

The system boundary and integration hub. Exposes every business operation as GraphQL queries/mutations and MCP tool endpoints. Connects to your existing systems (ERP, HRIS, document management, financial systems) through standards-based APIs. Each integration defines data scope and access boundaries — what flows in, what flows out, who can see what. Handles authorization, real-time sync, and multi-tenant access.

### Renown — Identity for Agents and Users

Unified identity management for every actor in the system — human operators, AI agents, external integrations, and partner organizations. Every action is attributed to a verified identity with defined permissions. Governs who can see what data, which agents can perform which operations, and how trust boundaries work when external parties interact with your systems. Designed as the extension point for enterprise identity integration: SSO adapters (SAML, OIDC) and directory service connectors are on the near-term roadmap, prioritized through co-creation partnerships.

## 5. Integration & Data Isolation

**"Connect everything. Expose nothing."**

*This is the section that answers: "How does this work with what we already have?" and "How do we let AI access data without losing control?" — two questions the CTO is asking simultaneously.*

### Integration is the starting point

Powerhouse doesn't replace your existing systems — it connects them to a structured data layer where AI can operate. Switchboard's standards-based APIs (GraphQL, MCP) integrate with your ERP, HRIS, document management, email, and financial systems. Integrations are straightforward to set up and define exactly what data flows into the AI-native layer and under what conditions.

### Every integration is a data boundary

Each connection to an existing system defines a scope: what data enters, what format it takes, and who — human or agent — can access it. An HR agent sees personnel records but not financial forecasts. A procurement agent sees vendor negotiations but not employee data. A legal review agent accesses contract terms but not compensation details. Isolation isn't a policy layer on top — it's defined at the integration level.

### Tiered AI model access

Not all data should reach the same AI model. Powerhouse supports graduated access:

- **Local models** for the most sensitive data — privileged legal communications, raw personnel files, board-level financials, trade secrets. Open-source models running on your hardware. Nothing leaves your network.
- **Frontier commercial models** for less sensitive but still confidential work — operating on censored summaries, redacted extracts, or pre-approved data shapes. The integration layer controls exactly what reaches the external model.
- **General-purpose commercial models** for non-confidential tasks — drafting, formatting, research, translation.

Your DPO defines the tiers. The architecture enforces them.

### The result

AI gets access to the data it needs to be useful — under constraints your compliance team can sign off on. Not "all or nothing." Graduated, auditable, configurable per integration, per agent, per data classification.

## 6. Privacy & Compliance

**"Built into the architecture. Not bolted on."**

*The section the CTO sends to the DPO, legal counsel, and compliance team.*

### Data residency

All business data is stored on infrastructure you control. Self-host on your own servers, or deploy on Vetra Cloud with contractual data residency guarantees. No data replication to third-party systems unless you explicitly configure it.

### Immutable audit trail

Every operation — human or AI — is cryptographically signed, timestamped, and appended to an immutable history. The audit trail is not a log; it *is* the data structure. Who did what, when, and with what authority is recorded at the architectural level. SOX, GDPR, HIPAA, industry-specific regulation: compliance is structural, not retrofitted.

### Data portability

Your data is JSON. Your schemas are typed definitions you own. Your application code is TypeScript. If you leave, everything comes with you — no extraction project, no vendor negotiation, no proprietary format to decode. The entire stack is open source.

*AI model isolation and agent sandboxing are covered in depth in section 5 (Integration & Data Isolation). This section focuses on the residency, audit, and portability guarantees that compliance teams evaluate.*
