What Renown Does

One identity framework.
Humans and machines.

Unified Identity Model

Every actor — whether an HR operator, a contract review agent, a Switchboard integration, or a partner organization — operates under the same identity and authorization framework. No separate system for managing agent permissions.

Cryptographic Attribution

Every operation is signed with the actor's cryptographic identity. The audit trail records not just what happened, but who did it — with verifiable proof. Agent actions are distinguishable from human actions at the data level.

Scoped Authorization

Permissions are declarative and granular: which data an actor can read, which operations they can perform, which documents and drives are accessible. The same rules apply to humans and agents — no special cases.

Access Control for Documents & Drives

Gate access to specific documents, drives, and workflow stages. Build approval hierarchies and checks-and-balances into your business processes at the identity layer.

Agent Identity

Your agents need identity too.

When AI agents change business data, your compliance team needs to answer one question: who did this, and were they authorized?

Distinct Agent Identities

Each agent has its own Renown identity, separate from the human who configured it. When an agent reviews a contract or updates a record, the action is attributed to the agent — not to a human.

Permission Boundaries

Agent permissions define data access scope, allowed operations, and AI model tier. Switchboard enforces these at the API boundary — the agent never gets to decide what it can access.

Audit Distinction

The immutable operation history distinguishes human actions from agent actions at the data level. Your compliance team can filter, inspect, and report on agent behavior independently.

Agent-to-Agent Trust

In multi-agent workflows, Renown governs trust relationships: which agents can communicate, what data flows between scopes, and how escalation from agent to human is authenticated.

Contribution-Based Reputation

Renown tracks verified contributions across the platform — project deliveries, document edits, workflow completions. Over time, this builds a contribution-based profile that informs trust decisions and access levels.

Enterprise Identity Integration

From crypto identity
to enterprise SSO.

Current Capabilities

Today: cryptographic identity with Ethereum-based authentication. Pseudonymous contributor profiles. Per-document and per-drive access control. Cross-organizational identity for partner collaboration.

Enterprise SSO Roadmap

On the roadmap: SAML integration (Azure AD, Okta, Ping Identity). OIDC for modern SSO flows. Directory service sync for organizational structure. For early enterprise partners, SSO integration is a co-creation priority — your requirements shape the implementation.
