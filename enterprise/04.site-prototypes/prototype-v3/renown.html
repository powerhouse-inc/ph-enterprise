What Renown Does

One identity framework.
Humans and machines.

Unified Identity Model

Every actor — human, agent, integration, partner — operates under the same identity framework. No separate system for agent permissions.

Cryptographic Attribution

Every operation signed with the actor's cryptographic identity. The trail records what happened and who did it — with verifiable proof.

Scoped Authorization

Declarative, granular permissions: data access, operations, documents, drives. Same rules for humans and agents.

Access Control for Documents & Drives

Gate access to documents, drives, and workflow stages. Build approval hierarchies into your processes at the identity layer.

Agent Identity

Your agents need identity too.

When AI agents change business data, your compliance team needs to answer one question: who did this, and were they authorized?

Distinct Agent Identities

Each agent has its own identity, separate from the human who configured it. Actions attributed to the agent, not the human.

Permission Boundaries

Data access scope, allowed operations, and model tier. Enforced at the API boundary — agents never decide what they can access.

Audit Distinction

Immutable history distinguishes human from agent actions. Filter, inspect, and report on agent behavior independently.

Agent-to-Agent Trust

Governs which agents can communicate, what data flows between scopes, and how escalation to humans is authenticated.

Contribution-Based Reputation

Tracks verified contributions — deliveries, edits, completions — building profiles that inform trust decisions and access levels.
